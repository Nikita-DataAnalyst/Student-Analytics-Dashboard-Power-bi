# Student Analytics Dashboard: Digital Addiction, Mental Health & Academic Impact

## 📌 Project Overview
This comprehensive end-to-end data analytics project explores the deep relationship between **students' screen time (daily usage hours), digital addiction levels, sleep patterns, mental health, and academic performance**. Using a global dataset of **705 students**, this dashboard translates raw behavioral data into actionable insights to help institutions identify "at-risk" students and understand the multi-dimensional impact of technology on youth.

---

## 🚀 Key Tech Stack
* **Data Visualization & Dashboarding:** Power BI Desktop
* **Advanced Analytics Features:** Trend lines, Matrix tables, Dynamic Multi-page Navigation, and Individual Drill-down Profiles.
* **Core Metrics Tracked:** 
  * Total Students Analyzed: **705**
  * Average Daily Screen Usage: **4.92 Hours**
  * Average Daily Sleep: **6.87 Hours**
  * Academically Affected Students: **64%**

---

## 📊 Key Insights & Dashboard Architecture

### 1. Mental Health & Lifestyle View
* **The Addiction vs. Mental Health Trend:** A clear negative correlation—as the **Digital Addiction Score increases (from 2 to 9), the Mental Health Rating drops significantly (from 9 down to 5)**.
* **Sleep Deprivation in Early Adulthood:** Sleep hours dip sharply around ages 18-19, hitting the lowest threshold before stabilizing near the average line of **6.70 hours**.

### 2. Academic Impact Analysis
* **Academic Level Breakdown:** High School students exhibit the highest Average Daily Usage (**5.5 Hours**) and the highest Addiction Scores compared to Undergraduates and Graduates.
* **Platform-Specific Impact:** Highly addictive short-form content and instant messaging platforms like **KakaoTalk (100%), WhatsApp (100%), TikTok (95%), and Snapchat (88%)** show the highest academic performance deterioration rates.

### 3. Relationships & Conflicts View
* **Demographic Split:** Balanced gender representation with **50.07% Male and 49.93% Female** students.
* **Social Stressors:** Single students (54.47% of the cohort) report the highest absolute conflict counts (**384 conflicts**), followed closely by those in relationships.

### 4. Interactive Student Profile (Drill-Through View)
* Features a dynamic, individual student look-up system (e.g., *Student ID 3: 20-year-old Undergraduate female from the USA, using TikTok as the primary platform, with a high addiction score of 9.00 and a deflated mental health rating of 5.00*).
